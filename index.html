SIP trunk
A dedicated digital connection between your phone carrier and interface.ai. Think of it as a private lane instead of public roads: cheaper per call and better quality, but your network team helps build the on-ramp (setup and IP whitelisting).
Porting your number
Your phone number moves to interface.ai's carrier permanently, the same way you keep your cell number when switching providers. After the port, interface.ai handles all the routing. Your team does almost nothing ongoing.
Forwarding your number
Your current carrier keeps the number and simply forwards calls onward, like mail forwarding. Fastest to set up. The trade-off: the "envelope" gets restamped along the way, so the original caller's network details don't survive the trip.
Question 4 of 4
How will the AI hand calls to your agents?
Ask your contact center platform team which of these it supports.
Not sure what these mean? Plain-English guide
SIP Refer
The AI acts like a receptionist who redirects your call to the right agent, then hangs up completely. Nothing stays on the line afterward, which is why it's a one-time flat fee instead of a running meter. Your contact center platform has to support receiving this kind of redirect.
SIP Invite
The AI conferences the agent in, then goes quiet. But the call stays connected through interface.ai's line for the whole conversation, like a receptionist holding the two lines together. That open line is why billing runs per minute while you talk to the agent.
Standard phone call (PSTN)
The AI simply dials your contact center like any regular outbound phone call. Works with any phone system, zero setup. The trade-off: the call travels the public phone network, where nobody controls the path, so quality can vary and caller details can get lost.
